Marthe Gautier (1925 – 2022)

“In 1958, Marthe Gautier discovers that an extra chromosome causes trisomy 21. Her discovery is published under a male colleague's name. She must wait 50 years for recognition.”

Jocelyn Bell Burnell (Born in 1943)

“In 1967, Jocelyn Bell Burnell captures the first signal from a pulsar, a neutron star. Her discovery revolutionizes astronomy. The Nobel will go to her thesis advisor.”

Lise Meitner (1878 – 1968)

“In 1938, Lise Meitner explains nuclear fission, the basis of atomic energy. Her calculations prove the splitting of the atom. But the Nobel Prize in Chemistry will be awarded to her colleague Otto Hahn.”

Chien-Shiung Wu (1912 – 1997)

“In 1956, Chien-Shiung Wu confirms a major theory in quantum physics: parity is not always conserved. A decisive discovery. Her male colleagues receive the Nobel, not her.”

Nettie Stevens (1861 – 1912)

“In 1905, Nettie Stevens discovers that the X and Y chromosomes determine biological sex. She writes it down, proves it, publishes it. But history remembers the name of another.”

Henrietta Lacks (1920 – 1951)

“En 1951, les cellules d’Henrietta Lacks deviennent immortelles : les cellules HeLa. Prélevées sans son consentement, elles sauveront des millions de vies. Son nom, lui, restera longtemps effacé.”

Rosalind Franklin (1920 – 1958)

“In 1952, Rosalind Franklin photographs DNA with X-rays. She discovers that it is formed of two spirals: the double helix. This image, Photo 51, will change biology forever.”
